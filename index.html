Visit Setif

Setif, laid out by the French as a grid of wide streets, is one of the highest cities in Algeria, and the remains of a great Byzantine fortress lie to the north. In 1959, a Roman cemetery was discovered.

Places to visit here:

Roman Ruins of Djemila

It was classified as a World Heritage Site in 1982. Its foundation dates back to the reign of the Roman Emperor Nerva in 96 and 97 AD. It also contains a museum containing a great archaeological treasure.

Babors National Park

It is classified as a protected area under the category of national parks. The Babur National Park, which contains an ecosystem very rich in animal and plant species, currently covers a total area of ​​23,656 hectares.

Byzantine Citadel

Basilica Quarter, Wall, Circus, Temple Quarter (Setif). A magnificent stone wall, largely preserved, surrounding a Byzantine fortress, built around 540. It has been restored and made available to the public.
